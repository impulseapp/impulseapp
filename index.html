When sightseeing your journey is your destination, so we completely reversed the concept of travel guides. In impulse, the beauty finds you!

The problem

Why neither traditional books nor current modern apps do the job.

That traditional travel guide books are still bought by people who are otherwise keen to use smartphone technology is an oddity. The last thing you want to carry around is an expensive and unwieldy brick of paper that requires the navigation skills of a mediaeval pirate and is not in any way tailored towards your needs. If you take a trip taking you through many plces, you would have to take an entire library!

One would think that there would be dozens of highly successful mobile apps capitalising on those disadvantages. Yet, all apps that have the ambition to replace physical travel guides have critical failures. Generally, there are two types of sightseeing assistance apps: maps and augmented reality. They fail to truly enhance sightseeing, because they are:

In a typical app-assisted sightseeing session watching (1) the GPS navigation arrow (2) will have your entire attention hijacked or (3) trick you into clicking points of interests without looking around to see the beauty around you. Or, in the case of AR, they trick you into constantly staring at the screen. And all that in a rather dull and (4) dispirited way.

We are all aware about how smartphones ruin social interaction at parties. Yet, we let the same technology take us hostage and remove seeing sights from app-guided sightseeing.

We want to take sights and seeing back into sightseeing. impulse is an app that allows you to fully indulge in the real world and set free your perception.

Impulses

We achieve this through subtle *impulses* that alert you whenever there is a sight worth seeing near you. Using *impulses* means you can comfortably carry your phone in your pocket without the fear of missing out on anything whilst slowly indulging in ambling in the alleys of an alluring town.

Seeing Sights - Not Screens

It is usually the free-roaming that is most exciting and memorable in any trip. impulse gives impulses to discover rather than just guidelines to follow. Be your own guide!

If you are interested, you can open up a detailed view of the sight in question. A picture will allow you to locate where it is. If there is more than one sight nearby, you can simply swipe to the one that interests you. If you still can't find it because something obstructs your view, you can view it on a map to find it.

Your Journey is Your Destination

We strongly believe that *your journey is your destination*. How often were you too caught up in navigating or widly swiping through websites or markers on maps that you forgot to experience the ambience of the city? **impulse** allows you to see the beauty around you the way you choose.

You can simply roam freely or let your interest be caught by some of the markers displayed near your location.

Easy Use

We built our app to be compatible with the Windows Phone user experience. Lots of fun swiping and a minimal yet intuitive interface.

Data Mining

We implemented several methods of automated content gathering: scraping from Wikipedia. Furthermore, crowdsourced polishing is possible.

Background Notifications

To remain as hands-free and non-intrusive as possible, the default way in which the app runs is in the background. Geofences initiate alert pushes.

Photos

Photos are great visual clues about the sight in question. They are used in several modes to aid finding the object.

Social Dimension

Challenge your friends to with quests or share achievements on Facebook. Customising routes is as fun as designing a level for a game.

Maps

If you need a little bit more assistance than the default spontaneous wandering mode, you can use the integrated Bing maps. Never get uncomfortably lost!

Quests & Routes

Quests and routes are paths chosen by other users or staff picks that are particularly worthwhile. Usually, they will be thematic.

Global

Because content-creation is heavily automated, the app can be localised for any country with sufficiently good databases of sights (e.g. Wikipedia).
